About Me

I grew up in Miami, Florida in a humble home, to an Immigrant single mother. It is here through my connection with our beautiful beaches, colorful sunrises, and distinct wildlife, where I learned the importance of our environment, and the unique challenges we face. It is up to us to take care of our planet and all living things. Keeping this at the forefront of my mind I worked really hard, because I knew I wanted to make a difference.

I was gracious enough to have moved to Boston, Massachuests to attend Northeastern University on a full ride Scholarship for resilent individuals who were able to flourish despite adversity. I pursed a degree in Environmental Sustainabiltiy to make sure I worked in a field that would help me make a positive impact on the world and help create a more equitable and sustainable future for everyone.

All my work experiences have been curated to focus on pushing myself, helping others, improving orgazational standards, either internal processes or internal culture, using my unique perspective, problem solving skills, and the most current technology.

I am passionate about collaboration, working with Community, and Improving our planet. I will admit it has been a current struggle for me to obtain work that is environmentally focuesed and fulfiling. Nonethless, my want to work within my community has led me to create meaningful projects which I've showcased below!

Projects

I am passionate about using my skills and experiences to make a positive impact on the world, and I am always looking for new ways to do so. Each project is a snippet of work I am helping push forward, born from my interests, personal time, and rooted in my need to do meaningful work.

Water Quality and Cancer Research

Collaborating with the Center of Cancer Research of Puerto Rico to better understand public water systems relation to different types of cancer. Building upon my previous research and integrating current medical data from the Center. Creating the most up to date information to inform communities, as well as enact change to better protect vulnerable populations.

Environmental Justice

Coordinating the Wampanoag Tribe in Massachusetts to secure native land and ensure environmental sustainability. Starting with historical knowledge, and government to create meaningful change for Tribal members.

Global Shaper National Campaign

As a Global Shaper member with the World Economic Forum our Austin Hub is working with the national Unshred Medical Debt non-profit to help relieve national medical debt. Fundraising locally in coordination with hubs across the U.S. to collectively relieve 10 million dollars of medical debt in 6 months. In addition, we are fostering community relationships at the University of Texas, Austin and Austin Community College to help young adults learn how to manage their finances, and make informed decisions about their money.
